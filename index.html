How to Read OBD2 Codes

To acquire the knowledge of reading OBD-II scanner codes, kindly proceed by following the comprehensive guide provided below.

  • Locate OBD2 Port: Find the OBD2 port under the dashboard.
  • Turn on Vehicle: Put the key in the "On" position.
  • Use OBD2 Scanner: Plug in the scanner and power it on.
  • Read Codes: Select "Read Codes" on the scanner to retrieve trouble codes.
  • Interpret Codes: Note alphanumeric codes and check their meanings through our Code Analyzer Below!.
  • Seek Help: Consult a professional mechanic if needed.

How to Clear Trouble Codes

To understand the process of clearing trouble codes, please follow the comprehensive instructions outlined below.

  • Access the Vehicle's Information using the OBD-II Scanner tool:

    • Use the scanner's menu or interface to select the "Clear Codes" or "Erase Codes" option. The exact wording may vary depending on the scanner.

  • The scanner will prompt you to confirm that you want to clear the trouble codes. Confirm the action.
  • The scanner will communicate with the ECU and clear the codes. This process may take a few moments.
  • Once the codes are cleared, the scanner should indicate that the operation is complete.

What is an OBD Scanner or Code Reader?

An OBD-II scanner is a car diagnostic tool that plugs into a port under your car's dashboard. It communicates with your car's computer to read and translate OBD-II codes, helping you pinpoint issues with your car's engine, transmission, or other systems, making it easier to diagnose and fix problems.

LIST OF COMMON OBD-II CODES

  • What it means: The P0420 code indicates that the catalytic converter in your vehicle's exhaust system is not working as efficiently as it should. It may not be reducing emissions as effectively as required.

    What to do: You can start by checking for exhaust leaks or damaged oxygen sensors. However, diagnosing and fixing this issue may require advanced tools and expertise. It's recommended to consult a professional mechanic for a thorough diagnosis and potential replacement of the catalytic converter. For replacement parts, consider AutoZone or other retailers nearby!

  • What it means: The P0171 code suggests that there is too much air and too little fuel in the air-fuel mixture on bank 1 of the engine.

    What to do: Check for vacuum leaks, damaged intake hoses, or a faulty Mass Air Flow (MAF) sensor. Seek professional assistance from a mechanic for a thorough diagnosis and repair. For replacement parts, consider AutoZone or other retailers nearby!

  • What it means: The P0300 code indicates that there are random or multiple cylinder misfires occurring in the engine.

    What to do: Inspect spark plugs, ignition coils, and fuel injectors. A professional mechanic can help identify the specific issue and guide repairs. For replacement parts, consider AutoZone or other retailers nearby!

  • What it means: The P0128 code suggests that the engine's coolant temperature is lower than the thermostat's regulating temperature.

    What to do: Check the thermostat, coolant level, and the coolant temperature sensor. Seek professional assistance from a mechanic for a proper diagnosis and repair. For replacement parts, consider AutoZone or other retailers nearby!

  • What it means: The P0440 code points to an issue with the vehicle's evaporative emission control system. It may indicate a leak in the system.

    What to do: Begin by checking the gas cap for proper sealing. If that's not the issue, consider seeking assistance from a professional mechanic for a more comprehensive diagnosis and repair. For replacement parts, consider AutoZone or other retailers nearby!

  • What it means: The P0717 code indicates that there is no signal from the input or turbine speed sensor in the transmission.

    What to do: This issue is related to the transmission. Seek professional help from a mechanic for a proper diagnosis and repair. For replacement parts, consider AutoZone or other retailers nearby!

  • What it means: The P0562 code indicates that the vehicle's electrical system voltage is lower than normal.

    What to do: Check the battery and alternator. Consult a mechanic for testing and replacement of these components if necessary. For replacement parts, consider AutoZone or other retailers nearby!
